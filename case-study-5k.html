How I Built a $5K/Month Automated Sales System

Case Study • February 2026 • 15 min read

The promise: Work less, earn more.
The reality: It actually works, but setup took 3 weeks and $200 in tools.

Here's everything — including the embarrassing mistakes and what I'd do differently.

The Before State

My situation (January 2026):

  • Solo consultant, 1:1 services
  • 8 sales calls per week (all manual)
  • $8,000/month revenue, capped by time
  • Hated prospecting, loved delivering

The problem: I was the bottleneck. To grow, I needed either hire a sales rep ($4,000/month + training) or automate.

The Setup: 3 Weeks, $197

Week 1: Foundation ($47 spent)

  • Day 1-2: Built landing page (Carrd, $19/year), lead capture form (Tally, free)
  • Day 3-4: Set up VAPI ($0.05/min), wrote conversation script (ChatGPT)
  • Day 5-7: Created 5-email sequence, set up Mailchimp (free)

Week 2: Integration ($73 spent)

  • Day 8-10: Calendly Pro ($10), Airtable CRM (free)
  • Day 11-12: Ran 50 test leads, fixed 12 bugs
  • Day 13-14: Added lead scoring, different paths for qualified/unqualified

Week 3: Launch ($77 spent)

  • Day 15-17: Soft launch with 100 existing leads, 23% booking rate
  • Day 18-21: Full launch with paid ads ($50/day), booked 12 meetings in first week

My Biggest Mistake

I thought automation meant "set and forget."

Reality: It's "set, monitor, optimize weekly."

First month I didn't check conversations. Found out AI was being too aggressive. Month 2 conversion jumped 40% after I started reviewing 20 calls/week.

What I'd Do Differently

  1. Start smaller: Lead capture + email first, AI calls Week 2
  2. Track everything from Day 1: Build analytics dashboard immediately
  3. Hire help for integration: Spent 40 hours on setup. Could have paid $500.
