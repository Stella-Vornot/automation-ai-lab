## The Setup: 3 Weeks, $197

### Week 1: Foundation ($47 spent)

**Day 1-2: Website & Forms**
- Built landing page (Carrd, $19/year)
- Created lead capture form (Tally, free)
- Set up Make.com account ($9)

**Day 3-4: AI Voice Agent**
- Signed up for VAPI ($0.05/min)
- Wrote conversation script (ChatGPT, $0)
- Recorded 20 test calls to refine prompts

**Day 5-7: Email Automation**
- Created 5-email sequence (wrote myself)
- Set up Mailchimp (free tier)
- Connected Make.com → Mailchimp

### Week 2: Integration ($73 spent)

**Day 8-10: Calendar & CRM**
- Calendly Pro ($10)
- Airtable (free) for CRM
- Connected booking → Google Calendar

**Day 11-12: Testing**
- Ran 50 test leads through system
- Fixed 12 bugs (mostly typos in webhooks)
- Realized AI voice was too aggressive, softened tone

**Day 13-14: Refinement**
- Added lead scoring (qualified/unqualified)
- Created different paths for each
- Set up notifications to my phone

### Week 3: Launch ($77 spent)

**Day 15-17: Soft Launch**
- Sent 100 existing leads through system
- 23% booking rate (vs 15% manual)
- AI was actually better than me at qualifying

**Day 18-21: Full Launch**
- Turned on paid ads ($50/day)
- System handling 50 leads/day
- Booked 12 meetings in first week

## What Worked

**1. AI qualification was surprisingly good**
- Asked better questions than I did
- Never got tired or impatient
- 24/7 availability = no missed opportunities

**2. Email nurture sequence essential**
- 40% of bookings came from email, not AI
- People need multiple touchpoints
- Case studies in emails = highest conversion

**3. Integration was the hard part**
- Each tool worked individually
- Connecting them took 80% of the time
- Worth it, but frustrating

---

## What Didn't Work

**1. First AI voice was too robotic**
- Used default settings
- Prospects hung up immediately
- Had to clone my own voice and add pauses

**2. Tried to automate everything at once**
- Should have started with just lead capture
- Added AI calls Week 2, email Week 3
- Would have saved 10 hours of debugging

**3. Didn't track data initially**
- No idea which leads converted
- Added Airtable tracking in Week 4
- Lost insights from first 200 leads

## The Exact Workflow

**Trigger:** Form submission on website  
**↓ 2 min delay**  
**Action 1:** VAPI makes AI call  
**↓ If qualified**  
**Action 2:** Add to email sequence  
**↓ Day 2-14**  
**Action 3:** Nurture emails sent  
**↓ If clicks booking link**  
**Action 4:** Calendly booking  
**↓ 24h before meeting**  
**Action 5:** Reminder email + prep questions  
**↓ Post-meeting**  
**Action 6:** Proposal email  
**↓ 3 days later**  
**Action 7:** Follow-up #1  
**↓ 7 days later**  
**Action 8:** Follow-up #2 or long-term nurture

## My Biggest Mistake

I thought automation meant "set and forget."

**Reality:** It's "set, monitor, optimize weekly."

First month I didn't check conversations. Found out AI was:
- Being too aggressive with pricing questions
- Not handling "I need to think about it" well
- Missing context on 30% of calls

**Fix:** Reviewed 20 random calls/week, updated prompts, retrained. Month 2 conversion jumped 40%.

---

## What I'd Do Differently

**If starting over:**

1. **Start smaller:** Lead capture + email first, AI calls Week 2
2. **Track everything from Day 1:** Build analytics dashboard immediately
3. **Use simpler tools:** Make.com is great but has learning curve. Zapier would be faster for beginners.
4. **Hire help for integration:** Spent 40 hours on setup. Could have paid someone $500 to do in 10 hours.

## Can You Replicate This?

**Yes, if:**
- You have a service/product that costs $1,000+
- You get 20+ leads/month already
- You can invest 20-30 hours in setup
- You'll monitor and optimize (not "set and forget")

**No, if:**
- Your product is under $500 (customer acquisition cost too high)
- You need complex custom demos
- You're not willing to tweak for 4-6 weeks
