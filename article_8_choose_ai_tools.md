## The Shiny Object Trap

**What happens:**
1. See cool AI tool on Twitter
2. Buy it immediately ($50-500/month)
3. Use it for 2 weeks
4. Forget about it
5. Keep paying for 6 months
6. Realize you wasted $1,500

**I've done this 8 times.** Here's how to stop.

---

## Step 1: Map Your Pain Points

Before looking at ANY tool, list your actual problems.

**Bad approach:** "I need AI for my business"  
**Good approach:** "I spend 10 hours/week on manual data entry"

**Exercise:**
1. Track your time for 3 days
2. List everything you do
3. Mark tasks that:
   - Take >2 hours/week
   - You hate doing
   - Could be automated
   - Don't require human judgment

---

## Step 2: Match Pain to AI Category

| Pain | AI Category | Example Tools |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Repetitive emails | Email automation | Mailchimp, ConvertKit |
| Scheduling | Calendar AI | Calendly, Clockwise |
| Writing content | AI writing | ChatGPT, Jasper |
| Customer support | Chatbots | Chatbase, Intercom |
| Sales calls | Voice AI | VAPI, Bland AI |
| Design | AI art | Midjourney, DALL-E |

---

## Step 3: The 3-Question Test

Before buying any tool:

**1. What specific problem does this solve?**

**2. How much time/money will this save me per month?**
- Cost: $50/month
- Time saved: 10 hours
- Your hourly rate: $100
- Value: $1,000 - $50 = $950/month profit ✅

**3. What's the risk if it doesn't work?**

**Only buy if:** Clear problem + positive ROI + low risk

---

## Step 4: Test Before You Commit

**Week 1:** Free trial only  
**Week 2:** Paid tier if promising  
**Week 3:** Evaluate & decide

**Keep if:** Using daily, saving time  
**Cancel if:** Not using, not saving

## Bottom Line

AI tools are powerful. But more tools ≠ better results.

**The winners:**
- Start with problems, not tools
- Test rigorously
- Audit quarterly
- Keep stacks small (5-8 tools max)

**Your goal:** 5-8 tools that save 20+ hours/week.
